Confidentiality

Confidentiality is a central part of psychological services. Information shared in therapy is treated with care and respect for your privacy. Personal information is not shared with others without written consent, except in specific situations required by law or professional ethical standards.

Limits of Confidentiality

There are important limits to confidentiality. Psychologists are legally and ethically required to take action if there is reason to believe that a client or another person may be at risk of serious harm. This includes situations involving risk of harm to oneself or others, concerns about the safety of a child or vulnerable person, or when records are required by a court of law.

Professional Standards and Record Keeping

Psychological records are maintained securely in accordance with the professional and ethical standards governing the practice of psychology in Nova Scotia. This includes the requirements of the Nova Scotia Regulator of Psychology (formerly the Nova Scotia Board of Examiners in Psychology) and the Canadian Code of Ethics for Psychologists.

Clinical records are maintained in paper format rather than through online client portals or electronic health record systems. Records are securely stored to protect client confidentiality.

Informed Consent

Before psychological services are provided, a thorough discussion of informed consent, confidentiality, privacy and record-keeping practices takes place. Clients and families are encouraged to ask questions so they can make informed decisions about participating in psychological services.
